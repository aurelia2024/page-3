Blueberry Cheesecake

    INGREDIENTS: Serves: 12
    FOR CHEESECAKE BASE:
  • 220 g (2 ¼ cups) finely crushed digestive biscuits
  • 65 g (½ stick + ½ tbsp) unsalted butter, melted

    • FOR BLUEBERRY CHEESECAKE FILLING:
  • 65 g (½ stick + ½ tbsp) unsalted butter, melted
  • 115 g (½ cup) full-fat plain or Greek-style yoghurt, room temperature
  • 150 g (¾ cup) caster/superfine sugar
  • 15 g (2 tbsp) cornstarch
  • zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 large eggs, room temperature
  • ½ tbsp lemon juice
  • ½ tsp vanilla bean paste
  • 250 g (2 cups) fresh blueberries

    • FOR BLUEBERRY SAUCE:
  • 250 g (2 cups) fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 50 g (¼ cup) caster/superfine or granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

    • INSTRUCTIONS: FOR CHEESECAKE BASE: Step 1:
  • Adjust the oven rack to the middle position, pre-heat the oven to 355ºF (180ºC) and line an 8 inch (20cm) springform pan with baking/greaseproof paper.
    • Step 2:
  • Mix together the crushed digestive biscuits and melted butter, then transfer the mixture into the lined springform pan and, using the flat bottom of a glass or measuring cup, compress them into an even layer with an approximately 1 ½ inch (4cm) rim around the edge.
    • Step 3:
  • Bake at 355ºF (180ºC) for 10 minutes, then remove from the oven and allow to cool until warm.

    • FOR BLUEBERRY CHEESECAKE FILLING: Step 1: Reduce the oven temperature to 285ºF (140ºC). Step 2: In a large bowl using a whisk, mix the cream cheese and yoghurt together until smooth. Step 3: Mix together sugar, cornstarch and lemon zest, and add them to the cream cheese mixture. Mix well until combined and smooth. Step 4: Add the eggs, one at a time, mixing well after each addition, until smooth. Step 5:> Add the lemon juice, vanilla and lemon zest. Mix well until smooth. Step 6: Add the blueberries and fold them in until evenly distributed. Step 7: Transfer the blueberry cheesecake filling onto the slightly cooled cheesecake base and smooth out the top. Step 8: Bake at 285ºF (140ºC) for about 50-60 minutes, or until the filling is very light golden brown around the edges, the edges are fully set, and the middle is still wobbly when you gently shake the baking pan. Step 9: Turn off the oven and allow the cheesecake to cool to room temperature in the turned-off oven with the oven door ajar. Step 10: Once cooled to room temperature, transfer the cheesecake into the fridge for at least 4 hours (or preferably overnight) before removing it from the baking pan onto a serving plate.
    FOR BLUEBERRY CHEESECAKE FILLING: Step 1: Add the blueberries, sugar and lemon juice to a saucepan. Cook over medium heat with frequent stirring until the blueberries release their juices and soften slightly. Step 2: Drain the released juices through a sieve and transfer them back into the saucepan. Transfer the blueberries into a separate bowl. Return the juices to the heat and cook until reduced. Step 3: Once reduced, pour the juices over the blueberries and mix together. Then, pour the blueberry sauce over the chilled cheesecake and gently spread it out into an even layer. Step 4: Chill the blueberry-sauce-covered cheesecake in the fridge for 30-45 minutes before slicing into individual portions and serving.
